Design Changes & Round 2

How we improved the prototype and what the second round of testing revealed

Design Changes

What Was Changed After Round 1

Based on findings from the first round of usability testing, several targeted improvements were identified and applied to the prototype before the second round of testing. Each change was directly tied to a specific issue observed during Round 1.

1. Simplified Navigation

Content was reorganized into clearly labeled sections with more intuitive headings. This addressed the issue of users struggling to locate key information within the module.

2. Improved Content Readability

Text density was reduced throughout the module. Long paragraphs were broken down using bullet points and concise explanations to make the content easier to read and process.

3. Additional Examples

More concrete examples were added to demonstrate how AI supports specific stages of the UX design process, including research, ideation, and prototyping. This responded directly to participant feedback that explanations felt too general.

4. Expanded Ethics Section

The ethical AI section was expanded to include clearer guidelines and realistic scenarios. Rather than abstract principles, participants were now presented with specific situations to help them understand when and how AI use is appropriate.

5. Redesigned Prompt Writing Activity

The prompt writing activity was redesigned to include step-by-step instructions and example prompts. This gave users a clear reference point and a structured process to follow when crafting effective AI prompts.

Round 2

Second Round of Testing

With these revisions in place, a second round of usability testing was conducted using a medium-to-high fidelity interactive prototype. This version included improved visuals, clearer navigation, and more refined content overall. Participants completed the same set of tasks from Round 1, allowing for a direct comparison of results.

What Improved

✓

Task Efficiency

Participants were able to complete tasks more quickly and with fewer errors compared to the first round. The clearer navigation structure made a measurable difference in how confidently users moved through the module.

✓

Understanding of AI in UX

Users reported a better understanding of how AI can support the UX design process. The additional examples and stage-specific guidance helped bridge the gap between abstract concepts and practical application.

✓

Prompt Writing Confidence

Participants demonstrated noticeably increased confidence when completing the prompt-writing activity, indicating that the added step-by-step guidance and example prompts were effective.

✓

Ethical Understanding

Ethical concepts were better understood in Round 2. The revised section, with its clearer guidelines and realistic scenarios, gave participants the context needed to apply ethical reasoning to their own work.

Conclusion

Overall Outcome

The second round of testing confirmed that the design changes successfully improved both the usability and effectiveness of the learning module. Participants completed tasks with greater ease, demonstrated stronger comprehension of the content, and reported higher confidence in using AI tools responsibly within UX design.

These results support the development of the final design, which incorporates feedback from both rounds of testing to produce a polished, student-centered learning experience.
