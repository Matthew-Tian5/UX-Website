UX Usability Study Overview Participants Tasks & Metrics Findings Design Changes

Participants & Testing Method

Who was tested and how the study was conducted

Participants

Who Was Tested

A total of 5 participants were recruited to evaluate the effectiveness of the AI-focused learning module. All participants were first-year UX students, as they represent the primary target users of the module. Participants had varying levels of familiarity with both UX design and artificial intelligence tools.

Recruitment Channels

  • UX courses
  • Peer networks
  • University student organizations
Testing Method

How the Study Was Conducted

A supervised usability test was conducted across two rounds. Participants were asked to interact with the prototype while thinking aloud, which allowed the facilitators to observe their thought process, expectations, and any points of confusion in real time.

Format

  • In-person (library or study room) or remote via Zoom
  • Each session lasted approximately 10–15 minutes

Approach

Participants completed a series of realistic tasks while thinking aloud. The facilitator observed and asked follow-up questions as needed. This method supports direct observation of user behaviour and helps surface areas of difficulty or confusion.

Round 1

First Round — Low-Fidelity Prototype

The first round of testing used a low-fidelity prototype that included a basic layout, navigation structure, and content outline. Observations were recorded during each session, and participants were encouraged to share feedback throughout.

Round 2

Second Round — Medium/High-Fidelity Prototype

After applying design changes based on Round 1 findings, a second round of testing was conducted using a medium-to-high fidelity interactive prototype. This version included improved visuals, clearer navigation, and more refined content. Participants completed the same set of tasks to evaluate whether the changes improved usability and comprehension.
