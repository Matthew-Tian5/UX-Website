Round 1 Findings

What We Learned from the First Round

Usability testing with the low-fidelity prototype revealed four key areas that needed improvement before the next iteration. While participants were generally able to complete basic tasks, several significant pain points emerged.

☰

Navigation & Layout

Most participants completed basic navigation tasks, but several struggled to quickly locate specific content within the module. The layout and organization of content needed to be clearer and more intuitive.

💡

Practical Examples

Participants responded positively to examples of how AI can be used in UX design — these helped users connect AI tools to real design tasks. However, several participants felt that explanations were too general and that step-by-step guidance would be more helpful.

⚖

Ethical Content

Ethical concerns remained unclear for several users. While participants appreciated that ethical content was included, they requested more concrete examples and clearer guidelines around acceptable AI use.

✍

Prompt Writing Activity

The prompt-writing activity was seen as both valuable and challenging. Several users struggled to write effective prompts, reinforcing earlier research that students need more structured guidance in this area.

Data Collected

Types of Data Gathered

Both quantitative and qualitative data were collected throughout each testing session.

Quantitative

  • Task completion rate (completed or not completed)
  • Time taken per task
  • Number of errors made

Qualitative

  • Participant verbal comments and think-aloud observations
  • Overall impressions and suggestions
  • Identified points of confusion in layout or content
Next Steps

What Happened After Round 1

  • Results were analyzed to identify the most common issues
  • Improvements were prioritized: navigation, content clarity, ethical guidelines
  • The prototype was updated before moving into Round 2
  • A second round of testing was planned using a higher-fidelity prototype
